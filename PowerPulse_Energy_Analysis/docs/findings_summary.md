
# PowerPulse Findings Summary
## US Energy Transition Analysis 2001-2022

---

## Executive Summary
The United States electricity sector is undergoing a significant energy transition.
While fossil fuels still dominate overall generation, renewable energy has grown
nearly 3 times between 2001 and 2021 — driven primarily by Wind and Solar.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1 — Fossil Fuels Still Dominate
- Coal is the #1 energy source at 33,732 TWh over 22 years
- Natural Gas is #2 at 23,302 TWh
- Fossil fuels account for more than 60% of all generation in every region

### Finding 2 — Texas is the Energy Capital
- Texas is #1 in total generation at 9,077 TWh
- Texas is also #1 in renewable generation at 116 TWh in 2021
- Texas generates more electricity than the next two states combined

### Finding 3 — Renewable Energy Tripled
- Renewable generation grew from 287 TWh in 2001 to 826 TWh in 2021
- That is nearly 3 times growth in 20 years
- Wind energy alone grew 56 times — from 6.74 TWh to 379 TWh

### Finding 4 — The West Leads in Renewables
- West region leads all regions in renewable energy percentage
- Vermont is the #1 renewable state at 99.73% clean energy
- Washington, Idaho, Oregon and Maine all exceed 70% renewable

### Finding 5 — The South Lags Behind
- South is the most fossil fuel dependent region at 28,523 TWh fossil
- Mississippi is the least renewable state at just 2.71%
- West Virginia generates 95.20% of its electricity from fossil fuels

### Finding 6 — Wind is the Renewable Champion
- Wind grew from almost nothing in 2001 to 379 TWh in 2021
- Cumulative Wind generation since 2001 has reached 2,913 TWh
- Great Plains states — Iowa, Kansas, Oklahoma — are Wind leaders

### Finding 7 — Solar is the Fastest Growing Source
- Solar was almost non existent before 2010
- By 2021 Solar reached 551 TWh cumulative generation
- Solar growth rate exceeds every other energy source post 2015

### Finding 8 — 13 States are Still Laggards
- 13 states generate less than 9% of electricity from renewables
- These states are concentrated in the South and Northeast
- Rhode Island, Connecticut and Delaware are surprising laggards

---

## Data Quality Finding
2022 data is incomplete — only 5 months recorded.
All trend analysis in this project uses 2001 to 2021 only.

---

## Recommendations
1. Southern states should prioritize Solar investment given high sunlight exposure
2. Northeast states should expand offshore Wind development
3. Federal incentives should target Quartile 1 Laggard states
4. Wind energy investment should continue — it has the strongest growth track record
